# Part 91 — General Operating and Flight Rules

> Educational summary. Fly GACA is **not affiliated with GACA**; always verify against the
> official GACAR before operating. This page helps you find and study the rule — it never
> replaces it.

## Subpart A — General

This Part prescribes the general operating and flight rules governing the operation of aircraft
within the Kingdom. It applies in addition to the operating rules of the relevant air-operator
Part. An air carrier conducting common-carriage operations must also comply with
[Part 121](./part-121.md), and a commuter or on-demand operator with Part 135.

Persons operating an aircraft for maintenance purposes must hold the certificate required by
Part 43, and the certificate-holder responsibilities in Part 119 continue to apply.

## Subpart C — Equipment, Instrument, and Certificate Requirements

No person may operate a civil aircraft unless it carries the airworthiness and registration
certificates required by Part 21. For instrument-flight operations, the navigation equipment
must meet the standard referenced in § 91.205, and altimeter-system tests must conform to
§ 91.411.

Operations in designated airspace are further constrained by Part 71 and Part 77; refer to
§ 91.303 for aerobatic-flight limitations.
