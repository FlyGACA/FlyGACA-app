Overview

What is Fly GACA?

A specialized Saudi platform that turns thousands of pages of scattered official regulation into a single searchable reference, practical flight tools, and an AI assistant that teaches you how to verify against the source — not how to memorize.

Mission

To be the digital flight deck for Saudi civil aviation

One place where a pilot, student, or instructor can find the regulation, understand it, and apply it — in their language. The full regulatory library is free forever as the top of the growth funnel; subscriptions, packs, and institutional seats are the value layers above it.

Status today

Built and shipped

The complete rebuild (React/TypeScript/Vite) is live and auto-deploys to production: the library and search, Captain Adel, 55 tools, the study hub, and accounts & commerce — all live.

The moment

Pre-revenue — by design

Paywalls are open today to build the base and trust. The commercial machinery (Stripe, seats, packs) is built, wired, and ready to switch on — funding flips it into growth mode.

The problem

The regulations exist — but they're unusable

As Saudi aviation accelerates under Vision 2030 (Riyadh Air, Saudia's expansion, new airports, rising demand for pilots), more people need to master the regulations — while the learning tools lag behind.

✕
Scattered and English-only. Huge PDFs spread across sources, with no unified search and no genuine Arabic version for most students and crews.
✕
Hard for beginners. No clear study path, no link between the regulation and the practical tool, and no fast way to verify a specific clause.
✕
No readiness signal. Schools and academies train from PDFs by hand, with no dashboard for who is actually "ready to sit."
✕
Generic AI is unreliable. It invents answers with no source citation — a real risk in a regulated field that does not tolerate guessing.
The idea / solution

One reference + tools + an assistant that cites the source

We turned the official regulations into clean structured data (JSON) — a real engineering asset, not scraped pages — and built three layers on top that make Saudi civil aviation genuinely learnable.

📚
Find it

The regulatory library

Every numbered GACAR Part + 205 global reference docs (ICAO/FAA/EASA) + 21 handbooks, instantly searchable — free forever.

🧭
Apply it

55 flight tools

From crosswind to route planning, fuel and atmosphere calculations — precise math, shareable links, all wired to Captain Adel.

🛩️
Understand it

Captain Adel (RAG)

An AI assistant (Genkit + Gemini) that answers "where does the regulation say…" and cites the exact Part and section — in Arabic or English.

Complemented by a full study hub: a 13-topic question bank, 9 Ground School modules, 7 study paths, a mock exam, and flashcards — with exam-prep packs for each licence.
