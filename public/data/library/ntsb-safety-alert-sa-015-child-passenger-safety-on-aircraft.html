Child Passenger Safety on Aircraft

Parents urged to use child restraint systems when

traveling with children

The problem

 Unlike when they are riding in a car, children under age 2 on an airplane are permitted to

be held in an adult’s lap and are not required to be in an appropriately secured child

restraint in their own seat.

 However, adults may not be able to maintain a secure hold on a lap-held child during

turbulen ce and survivable accidents. Preventable deaths and injuries have occurred in

children under age 2 who were u nrestrained.

 The Federal Aviation Administration ’s (FAA) official guidance emphasizes that the safest

place for young child ren in turbulence or an emergency is in an approved child restraint

system or device, not on an adult’s lap.

 The NTSB has investigated aircraft accidents involving unrestrained children and has

issued saf ety recommendations since 1979 asking the FAA to require that children under

age 2 be in an appropriately secured child restraint in their own seat .

What can you do to make traveling in an aircraft safer for your child ?

 Purchase a ticket for all children younger than 2 years and restrain them in a child

restraint system certified for use on aircraft.

 Ensure that infants and small children are restrained in a c hild restraint appropriate to

their size.

 Ensure that all children are properly restrained during takeoff, landing, and turbulent

conditions or when the seat belt sign is illuminated.
